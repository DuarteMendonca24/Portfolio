About this project

SHAZAAM (Science Hoaxes to Avoid Alienation in Generation Z, A Media Literacy Approach) aims to strengthen the critical thinking skills of young people, enabling them to assess the trustworthiness of scientific information and combat pseudoscience hoaxes. This project focuses on developing non-formal educational materials using gamification for both in-person and remote learning environments.

Adopting a techno-social approach, SHAZAAM combines social science methods with advanced digital tools. It operates in close consultation with academic institutions, scientific bodies, and civil organizations, complementing the activities of the European Digital Media Observatory (EDMO) and its hub, MedMO, which covers the European Southeast borders. The project targets 3,000 individuals in remote areas of Greece and Portugal. For more information about the project check the official website: https://shazaam.eu/

 Board Game

During my internship in MOG Technologies I was tasked to develop a board game in Unity from an existing prototype and implementing its mechanics. The goal of the game was to support the SHAZAAM project to combat disinformation caused by pseudoscience.

The narrative of the game consists on a group of characters that spread scientific misinformation. It's up to the player to unmask those characters and gains followers on social media.

Players can choose between two modes: Local Mode, where they share the same computer, and Multiplayer Mode, where each player uses their own computer.

In the lobby, players select their avatars and enter their names, preparing for the adventure ahead. The main map features turn-based movement determined by dice rolls, where players navigate through various cells with different effects.

Cell Types:

Normal Cells: No special effects.
Quiz Cells: Pose questions about pseudoscience, challenging players' knowledge.
Special Power Cells: Grant unique abilities to aid in gameplay.

The objective is to gain followers by correctly answering quiz questions and utilizing special cells. Each quiz cell contains a character token, with a total of five tokens to unlock. The game concludes when all tokens are collected. The game winner is the player with the most followers at the end.
